Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Here's a time line of Dr. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954 - He graduated in physics at Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, then affiliated with the University of Madras.
  • 1955 - He moved to Madras for studying aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960 - He joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service (DRDS).
  • 1969 - He was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) He became the project head of the country’s foremost Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1980 - As a Project Director to develop India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully injected the Rohini satellite in the near earth orbit and made India an exclusive member of Space Club.
  • 1981 - Receiving Padma Bhushan Award as research and development engineer scientist.
  • 1982 - He joined the Defence Research and Development Laboratory in Hyderabad as director after his stint with the Vikram Sarabbai Space Centre.
  • 1983 - Kalam returned to DRDO as its chief as he was asked to lead the ‘Integrated Guided Missile Development Program’ (IGMDP).
  • 1987 - Received Om Prakash Bhasin awards for science and technology.
  • 1989 - Indian scientists of the DRDO successfully test fired the Agni missile into the waters of the Indian Ocean. He was responsible for the development and operationalisation of AGNI.
  • 1990 - Received Padma Vibhushan Award and Successful launched of the "Akash" multi-target surface-to-air missile.
  • 1992 - Govermnent announced the appointment of Dr A P J Abdul Kalam as Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister, Secretary to the Department of Defence Research and Development and Director-General, Defence Research and Development Organization.
  • 1993 - The medium range surface-to-surface missile 'Prithvi' was launched from the interim test range (ITR) at Chandipur-on-Sea. Dr. Kalam was the Prithvi Project Director.
  • 1998 - Kalam received Bharat Ratna Award and also conferred the 13th Indira Gandhi Award for National Intergration by Congress President Sonia Gandhi.
  • 1999 - He lead the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in collaboration with Department of Atomic Energy, which made India a nuclear weapon State and also Appointed as Principal Scientific Advisor to GOI.
  • 2000 - He conferred doctor of science degree by the Madurai Kamaraj University.
  • 2002 - He was chosen the Presidential nominee by the ruling National Democratic Alliance (NDA), and he was elected the President. He became the 11th President of India on July 25, 2002 and served the position till July 25, 2007.
  • 2015 - Kalam went to IIM Shillong to deliver a lecture on ‘Creating a Livable Planet Earth,’ on July 27, 2015. While climbing a flight of stairs, he expressed some discomfort, but made his way to the auditorium. Only five minutes into the lecture, around 6:35pm IST, he collapsed in the lecture hall. He was taken to ‘Bethany Hospital’ in a critical condition. He was kept in the intensive care unit but lacked signs of life. At 7:45pm IST, he was declared dead due to cardiac arrest.
  • 2015 - The former president was laid to rest at Rameswaram's Pei Karumbu Ground with full state honours on July 30, 2015. Over 350,000 people attended the last rites, including the Prime Minister, the governor of Tamil Nadu and the chief ministers of Karnataka, Kerala and Andhra Pradesh.
